Why now

The documents that need AI most are the ones you can't share.

Whether you're a firm or a single person, the same bind shows up across law and medicine: the documents you most need to understand are the most sensitive, and every easy path either exposes them or leaves you stuck.

01

Public AI can waive privilege.

In February 2026 a federal court held that documents a party created with a consumer AI tool, then shared with counsel, were protected by neither attorney-client privilege nor work product. The prompts became evidence.

Ref: United States v. Heppner, S.D.N.Y. (2026)
02

Health records don't belong in a chatbot.

A care team risks a reportable breach. A patient trying to understand their own results hands them to a model that may keep and train on them. Either way, the help goes unused because the cost of exposure is too high.

Ref: HIPAA, PHI handling requirements
03

On your own, nothing protects you.

A large company can negotiate terms that keep its data out of training. Paste into a consumer chatbot as an individual and you get none of that. Whatever you type may be kept and reused, and you can't take it back.

Ref: consumer vs enterprise AI data terms
